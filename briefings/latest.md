## Status

US-Iran war Day 61 — large-scale strikes subsided into a Hormuz blockade, diplomatic gamesmanship, and Lebanon-front skirmishes. Ceasefire talks stalled; Iran's FM went to Moscow to meet Putin. Gulf states jointly condemned Tehran's strait actions. Brent crude holds above $111/bbl, 50% above pre-war levels.

## Other Fronts

**Ukraine** struck Russia's Tuapse oil refinery for the third time. Zelenskyy accused Israel of importing Russian-stolen grain via Haifa; Israel opened a probe. **Mali** — Defence Minister Camara killed in an April 25 home attack; JNIM/FLA launched coordinated northern offensives. **Sudan** — RSF controls el-Fasher after 18-month siege; ~375,000 in the worst hunger tier. **EU** approved $106B loan for Ukraine after Hungary lifted its veto.

## Key Developments

**UAE to leave OPEC** effective May 1 after nearly 60 years — third-largest producer exiting signals cartel unraveling. No immediate market impact while Hormuz blockade constrains Gulf exports, but UAE could ramp output postwar, lowering prices. Kim Jong Un held a memorial for North Korean troops killed fighting for Russia, praising those who "self-blasted" (grenade detonation) to avoid capture — confirming a previously suspected practice.

## Diplomatic/Military Posture

Iran submitted a proposal centered on ending the war and reopening Hormuz, but the US views it as deferring nuclear and missile issues. Tehran seeks a Russian economic lifeline under blockade. A 141-meter Russian superyacht cleared the strait despite the blockade. US sanctioned a China-based refinery and 40 shippers over Iranian oil transfers. Israel destroyed a Hezbollah tunnel network in southern Lebanon; an Israeli strike on a Lebanese army patrol killed 5, including 3 paramedics.

## Bottom Line

The war is transitioning from active combat to a blockade-and-negotiation phase — watch whether the UAE's OPEC exit accelerates Saudi market repositioning before Hormuz reopens.
